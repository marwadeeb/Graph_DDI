Model Performance Evaluation

Comparing graph heuristics, Logistic Regression, and GNN on the DDI prediction task.

Why cold-start evaluation is what matters here
The GNN's real-world purpose is to predict interactions for drugs with no documented DrugBank pairs — newly approved drugs, rare compounds, or combinations simply never studied together. In a standard random 80/20 split every test drug still appears in the training graph, so graph heuristics exploit dense connectivity (average degree 344) and score ~0.98 AUC — not because they generalise, but because the graph is so dense that almost any two test drugs share common neighbours.

In cold-start evaluation, 10 % of drugs are held out entirely — they have zero training edges. Common Neighbours, Adamic-Adar and Jaccard score exactly 0 for every cold pair (AUC = 0.50, pure chance). Logistic Regression survives because it uses node features (physicochemical properties, ATC codes, CYP450 profiles) that exist independently of graph topology. The GNN's goal is to beat LR by combining features with structural context from nearby warm drugs.

Evaluation Notes
  • Cold-start split: 10 % of drugs randomly selected at the drug level (not edge level), all their edges removed from training.
  • Warm split: 80/20 edge split matching the GNN training configuration, 1:1 positive:negative ratio.
  • GNN split: PyG RandomLinkSplit with 20 % masked positives (nnPU), 10 % val, 10 % test — same configuration as baselines.
  • Data: DrugBank Full Database v5.1 · 4,795 FDA-approved drugs · 824,249 documented interaction pairs.
